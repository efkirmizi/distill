\justifying

\begin{pblock}{1 \;\; Introduction}
Deep neural networks --- ResNet, WideResNet, VGG --- deliver high accuracy but
carry millions of parameters and billions of FLOPs per inference, far exceeding
the memory and power budgets of edge platforms such as autonomous drones, IoT
sensors and medical devices. This bottleneck is the \emph{memory wall} [1]:
fetching model parameters from DRAM eclipses available compute, making
aggressive compression an engineering imperative for practical edge AI.

Two compression families exist yet each fails in isolation.
\textbf{Tensor decomposition} (CP\,[2], Tucker\,[3]) reduces structural size but
causes vanishing gradients and accuracy collapse in the sequential
\emph{decompose-then-fine-tune} pipeline.
\textbf{Knowledge distillation} [5] guides a compact student with a teacher's
soft outputs but collapses at high compression ratios due to the
\emph{capacity gap} [6] and becomes structurally ill-defined once the
student's layers are tensor-factorized.
\end{pblock}

\vspace{5mm}

\begin{pblock}{2 \;\; Problem Definition}
Given a pre-trained teacher $\mathcal{T}$ and labelled dataset $\mathcal{D}$,
jointly construct and train two compressed students $S_{CP}$, $S_{Tuck}$ from
scratch to minimise the combined distillation loss:
\[\mathcal{L} \;=\!\!\sum_{i\in\{CE,\,KL,\,AT\}}\!\!\big(e^{-s_i}\mathcal{L}_i+s_i\big)\]
subject to:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Per-layer ranks $\{r_i\}$ derived by \textbf{EVBMF} on $\mathcal{T}$'s
        weight spectrum --- automatic, layer-adaptive, no manual tuning.
  \item Hint points $\mathcal{H}\!=\!\{h_1,\dots,h_K\}$ selected by
        \textbf{PURSUhInT} clustering of $\mathcal{T}$'s layer representations.
  \item Log-variance weights $s_i$ learned online (Kendall uncertainty) ---
        zero hand-tuned loss coefficients.
\end{itemize}
\end{pblock}

\vspace{5mm}

\begin{pblock}{3 \;\; Contributions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Joint, from-scratch} construction \& co-training of CP- and
        Tucker-decomposed students under one shared teacher --- replacing the
        fragile decompose-then-fine-tune pipeline.
  \item \textbf{PURSUhInT} clustering integrated into compressed-student
        training to pick non-redundant, semantic hint points.
  \item \textbf{EVBMF} teacher-guided per-layer ranks --- automatic, calibrated
        to the teacher's own weight spectrum.
  \item \textbf{Systematic study}: 8 teacher--student pairs, 2 datasets,
        5 distillation objectives, one identical pipeline.
\end{itemize}
\end{pblock}

\vspace{5mm}

\begin{pblock}{4 \;\; Automatic per-layer ranks (EVBMF)}
EVBMF reads the teacher's \textbf{trained weight spectrum} to set each layer's
rank --- no global ratio, no validation search.
\[ \hat{R} = \big|\,\sigma_i(\mathcal{W}) : \sigma_i(\mathcal{W}) > \sigma^*(\mathcal{W})\,\big| \]
\vspace{-3mm}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.99\linewidth]{figures/fig5_ranks.png}
\end{center}
{\small Markedly non-uniform: early layers keep $\sim$59\% of channels, deep
layers fall to 36--44\%. Tucker estimates input/output ranks
\emph{independently} per stage (Marchenko--Pastur threshold).}
\end{pblock}

\begin{pblock}{5 \;\; Proposed Framework}
A single joint optimization replaces the sequential pipeline: two compressed
students are \textbf{built from scratch} and \textbf{co-trained} under one
\textbf{frozen} teacher, then evaluated independently.
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig1_pipeline.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{0.5mm}
{\small \textbf{\color{itudark}One shared teacher forward pass} supervises both
students each step --- roughly \textbf{twice} the cost of a single distillation,
not three times.}
\end{pblock}

\vspace{5mm}

\begin{pblock}{6 \;\; How it works}

\begin{minicard}
\cardtitle{PURSUhInT \;\textbar\; \emph{where} to supervise}
Cluster teacher layers by representational similarity; each cluster centroid is
a non-redundant hint point ($K{=}3$ CIFAR, $K{=}4$ ImageNet).
\[ d(i,j) \;=\; 1 - R^{2}_{\mathrm{CCA}}(X_i, X_j) \;\xrightarrow{\;k\text{-means}\;}\; \mathcal{H}=\{h_1,\dots,h_K\} \]
\end{minicard}

\begin{minicard}
\cardtitle{Heterogeneous students \;\textbar\; \emph{what} to build}
Same base net, factorized two ways from the EVBMF rank map:
\\[2mm]
\textbf{CP} (4 ops): $1{\times}1 \!\to\! \mathrm{dw}\,k_H \!\to\! \mathrm{dw}\,k_W \!\to\! 1{\times}1$;\;
params $\to R(C_{in}{+}k_H{+}k_W{+}C_{out})$. Highest compression.
\\[2mm]
\textbf{Tucker} (3 ops): $1{\times}1 \!\to\! k_H{\times}k_W \text{ core} \!\to\! 1{\times}1$;
dense core keeps cross-channel correlations $\Rightarrow$ higher accuracy
\emph{and} lower latency.
\end{minicard}

\begin{minicard}
\cardtitle{Distillation + auto-balancing \;\textbar\; \emph{how} to train}
KD\,$+$\,attention transfer at the hint points:
\[ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{KL}}=\mathrm{KL}\!\big(\sigma(z_t/\tau)\,\|\,\sigma(z_s/\tau)\big),\;\;
   \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{AT}}=\!\sum_k \mathrm{MSE}(a_s^k,a_t^k) \]
Homoscedastic (Kendall) uncertainty weighting --- \textbf{no} hand-tuned coefficients:
\[ \mathcal{L} \;=\!\! \sum_{i\in\{\mathrm{CE,\,KL,\,AT}\}}\!\! \big(e^{-s_i}\,\mathcal{L}_i + s_i\big) \]
\end{minicard}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\linewidth]{figures/fig8_lossweights.png}
\end{center}
{\small Weights span \textbf{3 orders of magnitude} yet equalize the three
gradient contributions to within 10\% (CE\,0.90, KL\,1.00, AT\,0.91).}
\end{pblock}

\end{column}

\vspace{5mm}

\begin{pblock}{9 \;\; Conclusions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Joint compression\,+\,distillation \textbf{beats sequential pipelines}
        at every ratio tested --- Tucker within \textbf{1.6\,pp} of the teacher
        on ImageNet-100; both students within \textbf{3--5\,pp} on
        moderate-compression CIFAR-100 pairs.
  \item EVBMF\,+\,PURSUhInT\,+\,Kendall $\Rightarrow$ \textbf{zero tuned
        hyperparameters}.
  \item \textbf{Tucker} for accuracy \& lowest latency; \textbf{CP} for the
        tightest budget.
  \item Architecture-agnostic; complies with \mbox{ISO/IEC 23053:2022} \&
        \mbox{TS 4213:2022}.
\end{itemize}
{\small\color{itudark}\textbf{Future:} vision transformers $\cdot$ full
ImageNet-1k $\cdot$ post-training quantization $\cdot$ on-device energy.}
\end{pblock}
